Eclairs are the draw here that hooks many ($16 a dozen). Handmade, each one is slightly different: one a little broader than another, the icing on top thicker on one end, drippy on the other. But who cares. Each one is freshly made, the interior custard is dreamy, and the puffs are light and melt in your mouth. Owner Roula Kappas, the pastry chef these days, uses the original recipe from when the shop first opened on Boylston Street in Boston in 1919 by Kosmas Constantin, a Greek immigrant. It moved to its current location in 1929. Kappas and her late husband, Jimmy, bought the bakery from a cousin 32 years ago.
